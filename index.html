Why people reduce

The side effects that make people search for a way out

Long-term high-dose gabapentin can be effective — and for many people, over time it begins to cost more than it gives. If you're searching for a way to reduce, you probably already know this feeling. You're not imagining it.

This is not about stopping gabapentin. It's about reducing it carefully — under medical supervision — until the side effects are manageable and your quality of life is yours again.

Cognitive fog
Difficulty concentrating, memory lapses, the feeling of thinking through cotton wool — common at higher doses and worsening over time.
Emotional blunting
Feeling flat, disconnected, or less emotionally present than you used to be — a reduced emotional range that affects relationships and wellbeing.
Fatigue & weight gain
Persistent tiredness that doesn't improve with rest, and significant weight gain that's very difficult to reverse while remaining on higher doses.
Physical dependency
The anxiety when a prescription is late, the fear of missing a dose, the sense that the medication controls you rather than the other way around.

    Common questions

    Frequently asked questions

    Does my doctor need to build my taper schedule first?
    No — and that's the gap GabaDown fills. Based on your intake and published deprescribing guidelines, we produce a structured draft framework for you to bring to your doctor for review. You don't need a detailed plan from them before you start — just their general openness to reduction.
    Will my doctor and pharmacist get a copy?
    Yes. Every client receives a one-page healthcare team summary formatted for their doctor and pharmacist to review. Your doctor reviews the draft plan, adjusts if needed, and gives their approval. Your pharmacist sees the full picture for any supplement conversations.
    What if I need to slow down or pause?
    Hold guidance is built into every stage of your journal. Slowing down or pausing is part of a safe taper — not a failure. Any changes should always be made with your doctor's knowledge, and your tracking log helps you explain exactly what's been happening.
    How do I know if it's withdrawal or my original pain returning?
    This is one of the most important questions in any gabapentin taper and your journal addresses it directly. Withdrawal symptoms typically track closely with dose reductions and ease with time. Your tracking log is specifically designed to help you and your doctor distinguish the two. When in doubt, always call your doctor.
    Does GabaDown work for pregabalin (Lyrica) too?
    Not currently. GabaDown is built specifically for gabapentin users. Pregabalin has a different dosing profile and reduction framework — it's a related but distinct medication. We may address pregabalin in future, but every current journal is gabapentin-specific.
    I'm in Canada — can I use GabaDown?
    Yes. GabaDown is available to anyone reducing gabapentin under medical supervision in the US and Canada. Pricing is in USD. The deprescribing research it's built on is North American and international — nothing is US-specific.
    What if I'm not ready to buy yet?
    Download the free 7 Questions guide — it helps you have a more effective conversation with your doctor and is a good starting point regardless of whether GabaDown is right for you right now.
    But I need gabapentin for my pain — what happens when I'm off it?
    This is the most important question to ask before you start. Reducing gabapentin does not erase your underlying condition — and it shouldn't. What it does is help you and your doctor see more clearly what the medication is actually doing versus what your baseline pain actually is. Many people find their pain is more manageable than the medicated fog made it feel. Others work with their doctor to explore alternative pain management as the dose comes down. Either way, the taper creates clarity — and clarity gives you and your doctor better options than staying at a dose that's affecting your quality of life.
    Why would I want to reduce? It's managing my pain.
    For many people it is — and that's exactly why a careful supervised reduction is the right approach, not stopping abruptly. But long-term high-dose gabapentin is associated with significant side effects that worsen over time: cognitive fog, memory problems, emotional blunting, persistent fatigue, weight gain, and a deepening physical dependency. Many people find that as doses come down carefully, they feel more present, more themselves — and discover their baseline pain is lower than they thought. The goal is not to be pain-free. The goal is to be better off at a lower dose than you are at a higher one.
