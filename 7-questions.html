7 Questions to Ask Your Doctor About Gabapentin Reduction

Most appointments last 15 minutes. These questions will help you use every one of them — and show your doctor you're serious about doing this safely.

GabaDown · gabadown.com

Walking into a doctor's appointment without a plan is how tapers get dismissed or rushed. These 7 questions give you a framework — and reveal exactly where the gaps are that a structured protocol needs to fill.

01
I'd like to start reducing my gabapentin — what information do you need from me to support that plan?
Reframes the conversation from asking permission to starting a collaboration. Most patients never say this out loud — doing so signals you're serious, not impulsive.
02
Given how long I've been on this dose, how slowly should we go — and what's the smallest safe reduction increment?
Standard taper rates weren't designed for long-term, high-dose users. The answer to this question is specific to your history — and you need it before anything begins.
03
How will we know if we're reducing too fast — what are the signals to pause or hold at a dose?
Without a daily tracking system, this is guesswork. Your doctor needs data from you between appointments — not a vague sense that things feel "off."
04
How do we tell the difference between withdrawal symptoms and my original condition returning?
This is the question that derails most tapers. Without a clear framework, one bad week looks like failure — and patients stop. Your doctor needs a plan for this before it happens, not after.
05
What should I be doing to support my nervous system during the reduction — supplements, sleep, anything else?
Your doctor may not have time to cover this. But the gap between appointments is where most of the difficulty happens — and there are evidence-informed ways to fill it.
06
Can we document a taper plan together that I can refer back to between appointments?
Your doctor sees you for 15 minutes. The other 99% of this process happens without them in the room. A written plan changes everything — for both of you.
07
What support resources would help me stay on track between appointments — and who else should be on my care team?
This is the question most people never ask. And the honest answer is: your doctor can't be the only one. A structured support system between appointments isn't optional — it's what makes the difference between finishing and giving up.

If you find yourself unable to answer these questions — that's the gap. GabaDown builds the structured plan that fills it.
